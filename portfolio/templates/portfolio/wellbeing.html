Wellbeing

This page is a description of my mental health and work capability. Given out of work periods in the past, I seek to reassure a potential employer of my capacity to be highly productive. And I can be highly productive!

Work Hours

The full-time work I've done in the last six months in particular and the last 18 months where I've maintained timesheets, has given me a very good idea of my work capabilities. I can sustain up to 37.5 hours week (home working) and work up to a maximum 60 hours in one week.
When I am in the "zone", I can code for hours and not even notice the time passing, I'm enjoying the work so much. However, to be at my most productive, I would prefer hybrid, flexible annualised hours, with an average week below:
30 hours a week average


Mental Health

My mental health problems can be summarised as Complex Post Traumatic Stress Disorder (cPTSD).

I'm finding, less events are triggering replays of the trauma. This is partly due to changes in my life, effective medication and partly due to knowing the triggers. However, I am still capable of replaying trauma once or twice a year, which has the effect of tiring me and loss of concentration. This tends to last one or two weeks and will reduce my productivity over those weeks. This is why working flexibly, where I can build up hours, can act in lieu of the days where my mental health is poor.


Good Working Relationships

I am warm, friendly, honest and work well within a team.

The honesty about my mental health on this page may put off potential employers. If the price is it takes longer to find a job, then it is a price worth paying, as the job I will get, is one where I can be at my most productive, happier and the employer will be happy with my productivity.
